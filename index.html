Who I am

I’m a Scientist with 11+ years of experience in combined wetlab and drylab molecular biology research. For the past 7 years I’ve focused on RNA research. Throughout my PhD training and early career I’ve worked closely with wetlab scientists to write code for the processing, analysis and visualization of transcriptomics data. I’ve recently contributed to the development of eRibo, an eCLIP-based RNAseq technology used to study ribosome occupancy in conjunction with alternative RNA splicing patterns.

During my PhD training at the University of Pennsylvania, I also became immersed in the thriving environment of biotech commercialization taking place at the UCity Science Center and Pennovation. I've sought to stay in touch with the scientific portion of biotech R&D while also contributing to the commericalization of science, and working with sales and marketing teams to pitch genomics solutions to prospective clients. I’m actively looking for opportunities in biotech business development to enhance my industry rolodex.

What I am up to

Working to enable biotech in Puerto Rico.

As an independent scientist, I’m building bioinformatics research initiatives to further elucidate and leverage RNA regulatory patterns in oncogenicity, metabolic pathways, and degenerative disease. I'm currently establishing a new academic lab in San Juan, Puerto Rico , which focuses on decoding biology through computational methods that integrate multi-level omics data.

Furthermore, I've recently started building business a entitiy to empower biolabs studying disease with bioinformatics capabilities by contributing to the implementation of data processing pipelines as well as pushing scientific projects through custom analyses. The business currently focuses on providing products and services related to genomics research, but is expanding to include molecular dynamics research products.

Finally, to capitalize and systematize my work I've been building Ozcorp Scientific, an initiative to help scientific teams develop their biotech business and commercialize their technologies. Since graduate school I identified the need for an entity to help biotech venture teams with process implementation, product development, and operations management while scientists focus on what they do best: refining the science.



Born and raised in the heart of Puerto Rico, I have been deeply immersed in the island's enchanting culture, vibrant traditions, and warm community spirit. From a young age, I felt a strong connection to the land and its people, instilling in me a profound sense of responsibility towards its growth and development. As I matured, I discovered my true calling in the biotechnology field, recognizing the immense potential it held for transforming Puerto Rico's future. Driven by a burning desire to contribute to my homeland, I set out on a mission to enable biotech in Puerto Rico, believing that by fostering this cutting-edge industry, we can not only create economic opportunities but also advance healthcare, agriculture, industrializaiton, and environmental sustainability for generations to come.

My journey in the biotech arena has been one of perseverance and determination. Recognizing the unique challenges faced by the island, I have committed myself to bridging the gap between innovation and practicality, seeking collaborations with local universities, entrepreneurs, and governmental institutions. Therefore, I have sought to create an ecosystem that nurtured biotech startups, research initiatives, partnerships, and talent development.

Through tireless efforts, I am seeking to attract investments and forge partnerships, showcasing Puerto Rico as a thriving hub for biotech advancements. As I continue to work passionately towards this endeavor, my ultimate vision is to witness Puerto Rico at the forefront of biotechnology, making groundbreaking discoveries that benefit both the island and the world beyond.
